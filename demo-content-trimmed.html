Periodontal Disease

Periodontal Disease Treatment

Gum disease is one of the leading causes of tooth loss, but when caught early it can be reversed. Our team provides thorough periodontal care — from diagnosis through treatment — to protect your teeth and restore your oral health.

Call 931-388-2279 to schedule an appointment in our Columbia, TN office.

Gingivitis affects only the soft tissue of the gums. Symptoms are often mild but important to catch early before progressing to periodontitis.

Common symptoms include:

  • Swollen or bleeding gums
  • Bad breath or a metallic taste
  • Receding gums
  • Increasing spaces between teeth

If gingivitis progresses, it becomes periodontitis — affecting not just the gums but the bone supporting your teeth. Without treatment, teeth can loosen and may be lost.

Healthy Gums

Firm, pink gums attached to the teeth with strong bone support.

Gingivitis

Bacterial buildup causes red, swollen gums that bleed easily during brushing.

Early Periodontitis

Gums pull away from teeth, forming "pockets" that collect bacteria and lead to infection. Bone damage begins.

Moderate Periodontitis

Inflammation spreads, more bone is lost, teeth loosen, and gums recede further.

Advanced Periodontitis

A major cause of adult tooth loss, marked by painful abscesses from infection spreading beneath the gums.
